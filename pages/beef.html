Peek-A-Boo Beef Wellington

I'm calling this peek-a-boo-beef-Wellington, because what you see is what you get. All the components of a Wellington are cooked to perfection separately before assembling, and you get crispier puff pastry, mushrooms with a better texture, an exquisite tenderloin, and the prettiest stack of wonderful things you have ever seen or tasted. A perfect holiday meal for one.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4x8-inch) sheet frozen puff pastry
  • 1 (5 to 6 ounce) tenderloin steak
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons clarified butter, divided
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 large brown mushrooms, halved and thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced shallots
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves

Pan Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons sherry wine or brandy
  • 1 teaspoon sherry vinegar or wine vinegar
  • 3/4 cup beef bone broth
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon cold butter, cut into small pieces

Assembly

  • 1 tablespoon smooth duck pate
  • 1 (1 tablespoon) slice truffle brie cheese
  • 1 teaspoon sliced chives

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.
  2. Remove puff pastry from the freezer and allow to thaw on the countertop for about 5 minutes. While still partially frozen, use a 4-inch round pastry cutter to cut 2 circles of pastry; place on the prepared baking sheet. Use a fork to prick the dough all over.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Press pastry down with a spatula and flip over. Place back in the oven until deep golden brown, 10 to 15 minutes more. Transfer onto a cooling rack, and let cool completely before using.
  4. Season steak generously with salt and pepper and set aside.
  5. Add olive oil and 1 1/2 tablespoons clarified butter to a skillet set over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and a big pinch of salt, and cook, stirring, until mushrooms have given up their liquid and have started to brown, 5 to 7 minutes. Add shallots; cook and stir until they soften. sweeten, and turn translucent, 3 or 4 minutes more. Turn off the heat and add thyme leaves. Transfer to a bowl and reserve until needed.
  6. Place the skillet back on the stove over medium-high heat, and add remaining clarified butter. Once hot, sear steak for 3 to 4 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired doneness [120 to 125 degrees F (49 to 52 degrees C) for rare to medium rare]. Transfer onto a plate, cover loosely with foil, and let rest for about 4 minutes.
  7. Meanwhile, for pan sauce, pour sherry wine and vinegar into the skillet, and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Boil until mixture reduces by half, 30 to 60 seconds. Pour in beef bone broth, and season with salt and pepper. Continue to boil until reduced to about 1/4 cup liquid, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat to low; add cream and butter, and stir or swirl the pan until emulsified into a smooth sauce. Turn off the heat and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  8. Place rested steak on one piece of puff pastry, and top with 2 thin slices of pate. Spoon mushroom mixture over, and top with a thin slice of truffle brie. Spoon pan sauce over the top, garnish with a pinch of chives, and place second piece of puff pastry on top. Serve immediately.
